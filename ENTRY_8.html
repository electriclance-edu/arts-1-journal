What an interesting reading! It reminds me of my own reflection on Ways of Seeing some weeks ago, where presentation is such an intentful act in and of itself. The museum is intentful (sinful) in more ways, beyond presentation, into its own collection. The "primitive accumulation" the reading talks about is utterly drenched in the colonial mindset and the fetishization of other cultures as that which humanity (read: the West) had been "progressed from".

I have never been to a "natural museum" such as the University of Michigan's Museum of Natural History, but I do hope to see a museum that is completely consensual for once. Perhaps full of reproductions, not true "artifacts" stolen. One that respects the cultures it presents, and one that truly *presents* those cultures as what they are, not as what they might be in comparison to some prior standard as part of a message. Just ask your damn fellow humans if they'd be fine if you took a broom or coconut shell or two, and ask them how their culture should be presented, for once.
